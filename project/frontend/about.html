Our Organization

National Aeronautics and Space Administration

NASA's vision is to discover and expand knowledge for the benefit of humanity, pioneering the future in space exploration, scientific discovery, and aeronautics research.

Pioneering Space Exploration Since 1958

Established in response to the Soviet Union's Sputnik satellite, NASA has been at the forefront of space exploration for over six decades. From the Mercury and Apollo programs to the Space Shuttle and International Space Station, NASA has continuously pushed the boundaries of human achievement.

Today, NASA leads the Artemis program to return humans to the Moon and prepare for future missions to Mars, while continuing its vital Earth science research and developing new technologies for aviation and space exploration.

With centers across the United States and partnerships worldwide, NASA's work benefits people around the globe through technological innovations, scientific discoveries, and inspiration for future generations.

NASA's Structure

Major Departments & Centers

NASA's work is organized across multiple mission directorates and research centers, each specializing in different aspects of aeronautics and space exploration.

🚀

Space Operations

Manages NASA's spaceflight operations, including the International Space Station, commercial crew programs, and space launch services.

🔬

Science

Leads NASA's scientific research across astrophysics, Earth science, heliophysics, and planetary science disciplines.

🧑‍🚀

Exploration Systems

Develops capabilities for human exploration beyond low-Earth orbit, including the Artemis program and lunar Gateway.

✈

Aeronautics

Researches and develops advanced aviation technologies to improve air transportation safety, efficiency, and environmental performance.

🛰

Space Technology

Develops transformative space technologies for future NASA missions and commercial space applications.

🌍

Earth Science

Studies Earth's systems using satellites and airborne instruments to understand climate change and environmental processes.

Our Division

NASA BioResearch Department

Dedicated to understanding how living systems respond, adapt, and evolve in space environments to enable long-duration human exploration and improve life on Earth.

🧬

Space Biology Research

Investigating how microgravity, radiation, and other spaceflight factors affect biological systems from molecular to organism levels.

👨‍🚀

Human Research Program

Developing countermeasures to protect astronaut health and performance during long-duration space missions.

🌱

Advanced Life Support

Creating regenerative systems for air, water, and food production to sustain human presence beyond Earth.

🔍

Astrobiology

Studying the origin, evolution, and distribution of life in the universe and the potential for life elsewhere.
