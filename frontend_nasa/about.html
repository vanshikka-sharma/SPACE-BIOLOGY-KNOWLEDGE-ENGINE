Our Organization

National Aeronautics and Space Administration

NASA's vision is to discover and expand knowledge for the benefit of humanity, pioneering the future in space exploration, scientific discovery, and aeronautics research.

Pioneering Space Exploration Since 1958

Established in response to the Soviet Union's Sputnik satellite, NASA has been at the forefront of space exploration for over six decades. From the Mercury and Apollo programs to the Space Shuttle and International Space Station, NASA has continuously pushed the boundaries of human achievement.

Today, NASA leads the Artemis program to return humans to the Moon and prepare for future missions to Mars, while continuing its vital Earth science research and developing new technologies for aviation and space exploration.

With centers across the United States and partnerships worldwide, NASA's work benefits people around the globe through technological innovations, scientific discoveries, and inspiration for future generations.

Our Team

Development & Research Team

Meet the dedicated professionals behind NASA's biological research initiatives and the development of this digital platform.

Dr. Parag Vaishampayan

Division Chief, Space Biosciences Division

Dr. Vaishampayan leads the entire Space Biosciences Division at NASA's Ames Research Center in California's Silicon Valley. From 2020 to 2022, he served as a Space Biology Portfolio Scientist providing overall scientific leadership and direction to maintain the scientific integrity of projects within the Ames Space Biology Portfolio. He supported over 70 active space biology projects to better understand how spaceflight affects living systems.

Space Biology Portfolio Management Spaceflight Effects on Living Systems Scientific Leadership

Dr. Egle Cekanaviciute

Deputy Division Chief, Space Biosciences Division

Dr. Cekanaviciute serves as the Ames Research Center Lead for the NASA Human Research Program (HRP) and is also a Principal Investigator in the Radiation Biophysics Laboratory. She began her career at Ames as a contractor via USRA in 2017, joining civil service in 2020. She is also the course director of the STAR: Spaceflight Technologies, Application and Research training program.

Radiation Biophysics Human Spaceflight Physiology Scientific Program Leadership

Dr. Mary Voytek

Senior Scientist for Astrobiology

Dr. Voytek is Senior Scientist for Astrobiology at NASA, leading the Scientific Leadership & Collaboration program. She directs efforts in astrobiology, planetary science, and technology through analog research. Her work has shaped NASA's approach to exobiology and the search for life beyond Earth.

Astrobiology Planetary Science Exobiology

Dr. Joshua S. Alwood

Bone Biology and Biomechanics Researcher

Dr. Alwood investigates bone biology, biomechanics, and reproductive health in space. His research targets molecular mechanisms underlying skeletal changes during spaceflight to inform astronaut health and long-duration mission planning.
